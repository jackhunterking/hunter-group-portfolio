# Diagnostic Short Video Framework

## Audience

Target business owners and decision-makers who already spend money on ads, agencies, tools, or staff but are not getting the result they expected.

They are not looking for a beginner tutorial. They want to identify the person who can diagnose and fix the problem.

Use filter language that repels DIY viewers and attracts buyers.

## Role

Play diagnostician, not teacher.

Every short should feel like you looked at their business for 30 seconds and spotted the expensive problem they missed.

## Core Formula

Symptom -> Misdiagnosis -> Real Problem -> Fix

- Symptom: the hook and the visible pain.
- Misdiagnosis: what they think the problem is.
- Real problem: the reveal.
- Fix: the named solution or system, without teaching implementation steps.

## Structure Under 90 Seconds

### Hook, 0 to 3 seconds

Lead with the symptom, not the tactic.

Use specific numbers when helpful.

Use a slight contrarian angle.

The iPad should already show the first diagram frame.

Never start with "Hey guys" or "In this video".

### Diagnosis, 3 to 60 seconds

Walk through one pre-made FigJam diagram.

Use one problem only.

Use the viewer's language, not jargon.

Point to the working parts first. Then slow down at the broken part. Tie the break to money. Name the fix with authority.

### Payoff And CTA, Last 5 to 10 seconds

Summarize the fix in one sentence.

Use authority positioning, not a follow-for-tips CTA.

Good CTA patterns:

- "This is what we build for clients."
- "I fix this for a living."
- "Send this to whoever manages your ads."

## Topic Categories

- Meta and paid ads
- Landing pages and funnels
- Appointments and lead generation
- Client and agency problems
- Broader growth strategy

## Production Workflow

1. Batch 10 ideas.
2. Generate all FigJam diagrams in one sitting.
3. Use consistent colors: green means working, red means broken, gray means not reached.
4. Load diagrams to the iPad before filming.
5. Rehearse each video under 90 seconds.
6. Film back to back.
7. Edit for tight pacing.
8. Post daily or near daily.

## Example Angles

### Leads But No Calls

Symptom: "You're getting leads but nobody books a call."

Misdiagnosis: the ads are bad.

Real problem: the follow-up gap after the form submit.

Fix: a faster response and appointment-setting system.

### More Budget Is Not The Fix

Symptom: "Your agency says you need more budget. They're wrong."

Misdiagnosis: spend is too low.

Real problem: the landing page converts at 1.5% when it should be closer to 3 to 5%.

Fix: conversion repair before budget increase.

### Ads Worked Then Died

Symptom: "Your ads worked for a month then died."

Misdiagnosis: the platform changed.

Real problem: creative fatigue.

Fix: a creative testing pipeline.
